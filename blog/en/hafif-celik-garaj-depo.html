Light Steel Garage and Storage Buildings: Fast, Economical, Sturdy

You can order light steel without a house project. A vehicle garage, agricultural storage shed, workshop, or commercial storage building — the light steel frame system offers a fast, economical solution as a standalone structure too. We compare size, cost, and installation times.

🏘

What Is a Light Steel Garage and Storage Building?

Light steel garage and storage buildings use the same cold-formed steel framing technology we use in housing projects, applied on its own as a standalone structure. The load-bearing steel skeleton is manufactured at the factory and only assembled on site. The difference from a housing project: costly items such as interior insulation, plumbing/wiring, and finishing work are usually not required, so the build is much faster and more economical.

⚠ Important Note

Garage and storage projects can be ordered independently, without requiring a house project. They can be installed as an addition next to your existing home or business, on a plot of land, or as a single standalone structure.

Who Is It Suitable For?

  • Homeowners: Vehicle garage, garden equipment storage, seasonal storage.
  • Agricultural producers: Crop storage, machinery/equipment shelter, feed/fertilizer storage.
  • Tradespeople and SMEs: Workshop, stock storage, small production space.
  • Construction and site contractors: Site office/storage — a roomier, more permanent alternative to a container.
  • Commercial businesses: Expandable storage space for logistics and warehousing needs.

📊 Why More Economical Than Housing?

Garage and storage buildings usually don't need interior insulation, wiring, cladding, or finishing work. Even though the same steel frame technology is used, the m² cost can stay below the 8,000 TL/m² rough-construction figure we quote for housing.

Foundation Requirements

Light steel garage and storage buildings impose far less structural load than reinforced concrete buildings. As a result:

  • Small garage/storage (under 30 m²): A light grade beam or strip footing is sufficient — no deep excavation needed.
  • Medium-scale workshop/storage: A standard strip footing plus tie-beam system is used.
  • Large industrial storage: A raft foundation or reinforced strip footing is recommended based on the soil survey.

This significantly lowers foundation cost compared to housing — a larger share of the total project budget can go toward the frame and cladding instead.

Wall and Roof Options

For garage and storage projects, the most common and economical choice is trapezoidal sheet cladding — fast installation, low cost, and suited to flat or low-slope roofs. If an insulated storage building or workshop is desired, sandwich panel is more efficient. See our roof options article for a detailed comparison — the same logic applies to wall cladding as well.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many days does it take to install a light steel garage?

A small 20-30 m² vehicle garage is installed in 3-5 days once the foundation is ready. 50-100 m² storage/workshop buildings take 5-10 days, and industrial storage projects over 150 m² take 2-4 weeks. Because the steel frame is pre-manufactured at the factory, only installation takes place on site.

Do I need a building permit for a light steel storage building?

This depends on the size of the structure and the zoning status of the land. In many municipalities, outbuildings (garages, storage sheds) under a certain m² can be built with a simplified permit or permit exemption; larger storage and workshop buildings require a full building permit. We recommend checking your land's zoning status with the municipality before the project.

How much does a light steel garage/storage building cost?

A frame-and-roof-only (semi-open) structure costs 3,500-5,000 TL/m², an enclosed storage building 5,500-8,000 TL/m², and a turnkey, insulated, wired workshop 9,000-13,000 TL/m². These are more economical than housing projects because interior insulation, plumbing/wiring, and finishing work are usually not required.

Can a garage be installed on existing land without building a foundation from scratch?

Light steel garage and storage buildings impose low structural load, so they can be built on a strip footing or a light grade-beam foundation; a foundation as deep and costly as a house's is not needed. A stronger foundation may be recommended for larger storage buildings depending on the soil survey results.
